Cultural Sleep Practices You Can Use for Better Rest

October 23, 2025 • 4 min read

Photo by Unsplash

The Sleep Revolution: A Global Perspective

Imagine you're a night shift worker starting your day as others wind down. Around the world, different cultures have developed sleep enhancement techniques worth adopting—no matter what shift you work.

Key Cultural Sleep Practices

Spanish Siesta

Spain's traditional practice of closing shops from 2-5 PM acknowledges natural energy cycles. Even brief 20-minute naps can enhance alertness and cognitive performance for shift workers. The key is keeping naps short to avoid sleep inertia.

Japanese Inemuri

This cultural concept normalizes napping in public spaces and workplaces, reflecting an understanding that rest is essential. The practice encourages finding small moments throughout the day for rest—whether it's between calls or during a quiet moment in the station.

Scandinavian Hygge

This approach emphasizes creating cozy, comfortable sleep environments through soft lighting, warm blankets, and minimal screen exposure. Sleep sanctuaries benefit from blackout curtains and white noise machines—especially important when trying to sleep during daylight hours.

Ayurvedic Rituals

Indian wellness traditions recommend evening routines involving herbal tea, meditation, and stretching. Pre-sleep rituals signal the body to relax, with 30 minutes of calming activities proving beneficial. This is particularly valuable for shift workers who need to signal "bedtime" at non-traditional hours.

Mediterranean Diet

Whole foods, healthy fats, fruits, and vegetables support sleep quality. Nutritious snacks like nuts and yogurt help shift workers maintain energy without the crash that comes from processed foods and excessive caffeine.

Mindfulness Practice

Meditation reduces stress and anxiety, preparing minds for sleep. Apps offer guided resources for breathing exercises that can be done anywhere—even in a crew quarters between calls.

Actionable Recommendations

Here's how to integrate these practices into your life:

  • Schedule power naps: 20-minute naps during breaks can significantly boost alertness
  • Create sleep sanctuaries: Invest in blackout curtains and white noise machines
  • Establish pre-sleep routines: 30 minutes of calming activities before bed, no matter what time that is
  • Maintain healthy nutrition: Focus on whole foods and avoid heavy meals close to sleep time
  • Practice mindfulness meditation: Even 5 minutes can help transition your mind toward rest

Remember: the goal isn't to adopt all of these practices at once. Start with one that resonates with your lifestyle and build from there. Your body will thank you.

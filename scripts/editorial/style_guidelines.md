# SleepMedic Editorial Guidelines

## Audience (layered)
- **Primary**: Anyone struggling with sleep -- insomnia, schedule chaos, anxiety, bad habits.
- **Core niche**: Shift workers (EMTs, medics, nurses, firefighters) who can't follow rigid sleep advice.
- **Secondary**: Health optimizers, wearable users, parents, students, travelers.
- Write for the broadest relevant audience first. Add shift-worker-specific advice where it genuinely applies (don't force it).

## What good looks like
- Useful first, poetic second. Real help for real people.
- Varied formats and intros by week (see Template Rotation).
- Science-forward: name the mechanism, explain it plainly, then say what to do.
- Every post should answer one clear question a person would actually Google.

## Template Rotation (deterministic)
Choose one format per post using `isoWeek % 6`:
0. **Story-First** -- Lived moment, stakes, mechanism, protocol, reflective close.
1. **Science-First** -- Cold open stat or finding, explain mechanism, apply, checklist.
2. **Myth-Busting** -- 3-5 myths, evidence for each, what to do instead.
3. **Field Manual** -- Problem, quick triage decision-tree (If X, then Y), protocol, pitfalls.
4. **Q&A** -- 5-7 questions people actually ask, concise cited answers, mini-checklist.
5. **History/Philosophy Lens** -- Short vignette or quote (verifiable), modern physiology, practice.

## Intros (rotate with the template)
- Story hook (concrete, sensory).
- Data hook (one striking, sourced stat).
- Myth hook ("You've heard X. Here's what the data says.").
- Command hook (imperative line, then why).
- Question hook ("Why do hot flashes wake you at 3 a.m.?").
- History hook (one-line vignette, then pivot).

## Science Bar (every post)
- Include **3-6 evidence points** tied to named mechanisms (circadian phase, homeostatic sleep drive, thermoregulation, autonomic arousal, melatonin suppression, cortisol regulation).
- Cite **trusted orgs or primary reviews** (CDC, NIH, NIA, NAMS, AASM, ACOG, WHO, Cochrane). Link inline.
- Use **plain language** after each mechanism: "What this means for you..."
- No filler quotes. Only verifiable classics (Marcus Aurelius, Seneca) or none.

## Menopause-Specific Rules (when topic involves menopause)
- Cover vasomotor symptoms and thermoregulation disrupting sleep.
- Mention evidence-based options: CBT-I, cooling strategies, light management, exercise timing.
- Briefly note medical avenues (MHT/HRT, non-hormonal options) without dosing; state "talk to your clinician."
- Flag OSA risk increasing midlife.

## Practical Help (non-negotiable)
- Include **one** of: a decision tree, a one-week protocol (AM/PM actions), or a checklist + tracker.
- Write steps as commands. 3-7 items. Make success measurable (e.g., "Wake at 06:30 +/- 15 min for 7 days").
- Add "If this, then that" branches for common constraints (shift workers, parents, travelers).

## App Mentions (subtle, optional)
- Maximum ONE natural app mention per post, only when the topic genuinely relates to a SleepMedic feature.
- Mention the CATEGORY of tool first ("sleep tracking apps", "adaptive sleep tools"), then SleepMedic as one example.
- Never in the first paragraph. Never in the sources section.
- Never salesy. Never "best app" or "download now." Just factual.
- If the topic has no natural connection to an app feature, skip it entirely.

## Links
- **4-8 inline links** from the whitelist only: NAMS, NIH/NIA, AASM, ACOG, CDC, WHO, Cochrane, NICE.
- Internal links to related existing posts where genuinely relevant (1-3 max).

## Formatting
- Title: 6-10 words. Concrete benefit or curiosity. No clickbait, no "Ultimate Guide."
- Length: ~700-1,000 words.
- End with: Checklist or Protocol, Sources, Brief disclaimer, Grounded closing line.

## Tone
- Direct, human, expert, practical. No corporate buzzwords. No "optimize" or "leverage."
- Close with a short, grounded line. Example: "Rest well. Rise ready."
